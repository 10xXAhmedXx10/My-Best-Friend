German shepherd

The German Shepherd is a breed of working dog known for its intelligence and versatility as a police, service and working dog. It's one of the most popular breeds worldwide.
Size: Adult German Shepherds stand at 24 to 26 inches at the shoulder for males, and 22 to 24 inches for females. Weight ranges from 65 to 90 pounds in males, and 50 to 70 pounds in females.
Appearance: German Shepherds are large, athletic, and muscular dogs with a domed forehead, a long square-cut muzzle and strong jaws. They have medium-sized, brown eyes, and their ears are large and stand erect.
Coat and Color: Their double coat is close and dense with a thick undercoat. It can be short or long and is most often tan and black, or red and black in color, but can also be all black or all white, although this is less common.
Personality: German Shepherds are highly intelligent, confident, and courageous dogs with a keen sense of duty and dedication. They are often aloof with strangers, but generally kind and loving with their family. They are excellent with children and make great family pets, provided they are properly trained and socialized.
Exercise and Health: German Shepherds require lots of exercise for their physical and mental well-being. They enjoy activities such as fetch, agility courses, running, and swimming. Health-wise, they are generally healthy but are prone to certain genetic conditions such as hip and elbow dysplasia.
Training: Due to their intelligence and versatility, German Shepherds are easy to train. They are quick to learn commands, which is why they are often used for roles in search and rescue, detection, and as guide dogs for the blind.
Overall, German Shepherds are loyal, protective, and eager to please. They need consistent training, socialization, and exercise, and in return, they offer companionship and unwavering loyalty to their families.
